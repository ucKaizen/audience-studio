The pain we already know

Brand Lift, Sales Lift, copy test, MMM today

  • Lift studies and copy tests take weeks. Clean-room jobs take longer.
  • Sample collapses in the audience cells we most want to measure.
  • Identifier resolution between exposure and outcome is bilateral, legal-heavy, slow.
  • Many decisions never get a measurement at all — cell too thin, question too small to fund.
Headline questions are well served today. Specific questions are not.

A spectrum of use — from gap-fill to full simulation

Run alongside a real study, plug a missing piece, or replace it entirely.

Mode A

Gap-fill

Run alongside an existing study to plug a missing piece.

When to use

  • Cells too thin to report
  • Scenario not tested by the study
  • Variant not included in the original brief
  • Forward-looking forecast where data does not yet exist
Mode B

Full simulation

Run as a standalone study when a real panel isn't feasible.

When to use

  • Triage and pre-flight before commissioning a panel
  • Exploratory question where speed beats fidelity
  • No real panel commissionable — too niche, sensitive, or future
  • Scenario rehearsal: “what would happen if…”

What we measure to know it works

Three tiers of validation, not one number

Graph QC

  • Coverage of population segments
  • Entity hygiene (dedup, schema)
  • Aggregate marginal fit vs panel
  • Freshness lag
  • Month-on-month drift

Agent QC

  • Persona–panel alignment
  • Determinism (seed-stable)
  • Within-cohort diversity
  • Cross-LLM robustness

Simulation QC

  • Sim-vs-peg residual
  • Calibration plot (band coverage)
  • Lift over naïve baseline
  • Residual stability over time

Each tier has its own audit cadence and its own pass/fail thresholds.

Agents

Census pool · synthetic fit · library curation

  • Census-scale pool projected deterministically from the substrate. Acts as a control, not a runtime universe.
  • Synthetic-fit subset selected per question with declared criteria. A contrarian counterfactual subset runs alongside as a bias-laundering check.
  • Library curation by retrospective fit on known-truth scenarios. Indexed by decision class. Re-validated on a cadence.

Risks & unknowns

The non-believer section — the things we will be asked

  1. Consent & second-use. Panel consent envelope may not cover agent projection. Legal review pending.
  2. Bias laundering. Subset selection can hardwire the answer. Mitigation: declared criteria + contrarian counterfactual subset.
  3. Cross-class transferability. Calibration on one signal does not transfer free to another.
  4. Regime change. Validated subsets decay. Library needs scheduled re-validation.
  5. Aggregate-only validity. Individual agents are not the product.
  6. Engine ceiling. Price/distribution-conditional purchase is not yet modelled cleanly.
  7. LLM drift. Model upgrades move numbers. Mitigated by cross-LLM robustness checks + frozen seeds + cache.
  8. Circularity. Panel both builds and validates. Mitigated by held-out validation slices.
  9. Demoware market risk. Saturated with "AI consumer" demos. Differentiation rests on the substrate.
  10. Commercial model. Per-study, per-scenario, per-decision pricing is unsettled.

What would falsify this

A pre-committed exit criterion per decision class

For every calibrated decision class, we commit in advance to:

  • A declared residual band — expected sim-vs-peg error
  • A declared coverage rate — % of calibration runs whose confidence band contains truth
  • A declared stability window — how long the residual can stay flat before re-validation

If a class fails any of the three across a defined audit window, it is de-listed from the calibration matrix. Sims for that class are paused until reset.

The matrix shrinks as well as grows. That is the discipline that distinguishes this from demoware.
